What research is done with the RIDataHUB?

Research/Overview content

DataSpark’s primary objectives for the DataHUB are:

  • to maintain continuously linked datasets for use by researchers and state agencies,
  • to generate analyses and reports using those datasets for policymakers, and
  • to support research and analysis for the public good.

See current and past research projects created using the RIDataHUB.

Support Government Agencies

The RI DataHUB has partnered with many state and local government agencies across Rhode Island to help support programs and inform decision-making. DataSpark partners with the state based on the services needed and has undertaken state work as a research subgrantee and as a data services vendor. Data Stories resulting from some of these partnerships are viewable at ridatahub.org/datastories.

DataSpark’s work using the RI DataHUB is always driven by state priorities and is either commissioned by the state or undertaken with state approval. Learn more about data use oversight.

Support Research in Institutions of Higher Education

Linked, de-identified data from the RI DataHUB can be used by approved researchers under strict security and confidentiality requirements and agency consent. DataSpark works with researchers who have proposed an analysis that aligns with state research priorities. DataSpark staff help researchers understand how to translate the DataHUB’s administrative data into the analytic data. If proposed analyses are approved by agency data providers, DataSpark contracts with researchers to prepare and securely transmit the requested data for use during the approved project period.

Past and current projects have included research on the economic and social impact of Adult Education Programs in Rhode Island, research on the labor market outcomes to workforce training programs, research on the association between childhood lead exposure and later school outcomes, and research on the postsecondary outcomes to K-12 career and technical education.

Enable Cross-Agency Data Integration

Along with data infrastructure and data products, the RI DataHUB helps build strategic partnerships to support continued data integration in RI. Through the Governor’s State Data Strategy Initiative, we are working to help develop statewide data management and identify processes to enable more efficient, more secure uses of administrative data. Our knowledge about data governance has been tapped by this and other efforts.

The DataHUB is also a source of innovation on the technical aspects of data integration, including improvements to data linkage, data quality, and data documentation. Click the links below to access information on some of the cross-agency data integration efforts we’ve been involved in.

Enable Policy and Program Evaluation

The RI DataHUB has collaborated with the RI Department of Health, researchers from Brown University, the Urban Institute, HousingWorks RI, and many others to evaluate policy related to housing, adult education, school-based interventions, and childhood lead poisoning. Unlike researcher-led projects, policy and program evaluation help to assess support and compliance with existing policies and programs, identify gaps or overlaps in implementation, demonstrate the impacts and value of a policy or program, and provide accountability for resources invested by states or organizations. The findings feed directly back to decision-makers.

As an SLDS, the RI DataHUB can uniquely support such efforts because newly-collected data can be merged with existing administrative records for more cost-effective “quasi-experimental” analyses.

Past projects have included analyses of academic outcomes for various types of school-based interventions, such as after-school, college readiness, or restorative justice programming; investigations into access to early education services among children from disadvantaged populations; and tracking the college majors selected by Rhode Islanders according to their high school preparedness in computer science.
